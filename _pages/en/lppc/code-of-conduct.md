The LPPC Code of Conduct defines the minimum conditions for participation in meetings, digital channels, and activities associated with the community. It exists to protect the study environment, coexistence among participants, and the public image of the people, companies, and institutions involved.

## Respect And Coexistence

All people must be treated with respect. Discriminatory practices, harassment, intimidation, or disrespect based on gender, race, sexual orientation, gender identity, origin, disability, religion, age, or any other personal or institutional condition will not be tolerated.

Technical discussions may be firm, but they must remain guided by arguments, evidence, and learning. Corrections should help improve solutions and reasoning, not embarrass participants.

## People, Companies, And Institutions

Participants connected to schools, educational institutions, companies, or other organizations must treat those names with the highest consideration in any public statement. LPPC does not accept offenses against people, companies, or institutions.

Academic or competitive rivalry can serve as a healthy incentive for preparation. It must not be used for personal disputes, personal attacks, institutional disqualification, or hostility between groups.

## Authorship And Study Integrity

LPPC values the process of reasoning, attempting, making mistakes, reviewing, and discussing. Participants must be able to explain the solutions they present, including main ideas, implementation choices, and limitations.

Copying solutions without understanding, omitting sources, or presenting third-party work as one's own harms technical formation. Solution discussion is welcome when it preserves authorship, learning, and intellectual honesty.

## Use Of Generative AI

Generative AI tools may be used as study support, for example to review concepts, suggest reading paths, or help compare approaches after a real attempt. They must not replace the logical, mathematical, and algorithmic reasoning that people need to develop.

LPPC does not accept solutions entirely produced by generative AI as a substitute for human work in understanding, modeling, and implementation. It also does not encourage outsourcing computational thinking to automated systems.

When a tool is used in a relevant way during study or solution preparation, the participant should remain transparent about that use. The central criterion is simple: the person must understand, justify, and defend the solution presented.

## Security Environments

In the cybersecurity track, all practice must occur only in authorized environments, personal labs, or platforms created for training. LPPC does not support intrusions, tests without permission, or any conduct that violates laws, institutional rules, or third-party rights.

## Enforcement

Disrespectful situations may lead to guidance, warning, message removal, participation restriction, or exclusion from LPPC channels. The response should consider the gravity of the case, protection of the people involved, and preservation of the study environment.
