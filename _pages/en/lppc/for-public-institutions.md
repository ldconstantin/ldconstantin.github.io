Public institutions can support LPPC by strengthening access for children, adolescents, students, and local communities to activities involving reasoning, programming, and problem solving. This support can happen without creating a broad formal affiliation, provided there is authorization, prior organization, and respect for the initiative's open character.

LPPC can dialogue with public schools, departments, libraries, youth centers, cultural spaces, public universities, federal institutes, and agencies interested in education, science, technology, and digital inclusion. Collaboration should prioritize formative and publicly responsible activities.

## Forms Of Collaboration

- Host meetings, workshops, simulations, talks, and discussion sessions.
- Disseminate LPPC activities in schools, libraries, community centers, and institutional channels.
- Encourage children and adolescents to participate in competitions such as OBM, OBMEP, OBI, and similar initiatives.
- Support introductory events on logical reasoning, programming, and problem solving.
- Provide spaces for study meetings, team preparation, and technical discussion.
- Bring together teachers, students, and professionals interested in scientific olympiads and competitive programming.
- Support regional actions connecting schools, higher-education institutions, companies, and technical communities.

## Institutional Care

LPPC must preserve its independence, voluntary character, and formative purpose. Collaborations with public institutions should be communicated clearly, without suggesting formal partnership when there is only occasional support.

Activities involving children and adolescents require special attention to authorization, safety, communication with guardians, and the rules of the institutions involved. LPPC must always act in a way compatible with local rules and participant protection.
