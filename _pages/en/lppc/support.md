LPPC is a voluntary, independent, and non-profit project. It relies on the support of students, teachers, enthusiasts, and computing professionals to maintain meetings, tracks, communication channels, and public materials.

Supporting LPPC does not require a broad or permanent commitment. Many contributions can be occasional, such as hosting a meeting, proposing an activity, reviewing a track, or helping disseminate a public call.

## Forms Of Support

- Host meetings, workshops, simulations, or discussion sessions.
- Propose events, activities, and challenges compatible with LPPC's formative character.
- Help design competitions, study tracks, and problem lists.
- Contribute to the maintenance of this website and, in the future, of a possible dedicated LPPC website.
- Moderate Discord discussion channels with seriousness, respect, and regularity.
- Organize links, resources, and study materials by track.
- Disseminate LPPC through social media, institutional boards, academic groups, and technical communities.
- Help beginners find a first study track without replacing each participant's own effort.

## Companies

Companies can support LPPC by providing spaces for meetings, disseminating activities internally, encouraging professionals and interns to participate, and suggesting technical challenges compatible with the community's formation.

The expected benefit is indirect and formative. Participants who train with problems develop precise reading, logical reasoning, implementation discipline, teamwork, and the ability to analyze mistakes.

Challenges suggested by companies must respect LPPC's educational purpose. They must not turn the community into free service work, an informal hiring process, or a marketing campaign.

## Educational Institutions

High schools, technical schools, federal institutes, colleges, university centers, and universities can collaborate by publicizing meetings, encouraging teams, and hosting activities when space is available.

LPPC can contribute to talent formation, stronger logical-mathematical reasoning, and team preparation for competitions such as OBI, Maratona SBC de Programação, and MFP. These results depend on continuity, regular study, and responsible participation.

Institutional support does not need to represent a formal partnership. It can simply be a concrete way to encourage an open educational activity.

## Public Institutions

Public institutions can collaborate by strengthening access for children, adolescents, students, and local communities to activities involving reasoning, programming, and problem solving.

Examples include hosting workshops, disseminating activities in schools and libraries, encouraging participation in competitions such as OBM, OBMEP, and OBI, and bringing together teachers, students, and professionals interested in scientific olympiads and competitive programming.

Activities involving children and adolescents require special attention to authorization, safety, communication with guardians, and the rules of the institutions involved. LPPC must act in a way compatible with local rules and participant protection.

## How To Propose Support

The best initial path is to join the [LPPC Discord]({{ site.data.lppc.links.discord }}) and present the proposal clearly. State the type of support, the expected audience, the approximate duration, and the practical conditions required.

Proposals must respect the [Code of Conduct](/en/lppc/code-of-conduct/) and preserve LPPC's voluntary, open, and formative character. Activities must not turn the community into free service work, a marketing campaign, or an informal hiring process.
