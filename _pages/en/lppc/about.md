The **Passo Fundo Competitive Programming League** is an independent and open initiative dedicated to algorithms, data structures, and problem solving. LPPC is headquartered in Passo Fundo and aims to bring together people interested in regular competitive programming practice.

The initiative does not replace courses, disciplines, or institutional projects. It works as a complementary space for training, discussion, and technical formation, with participation open to high-school, technical, undergraduate, and professional audiences in the region.

Meetings are held preferably in educational institutions and companies in the region, always subject to availability, authorization, and prior organization. When an in-person meeting is not viable, the activity may occur remotely.

Passo Fundo has conditions to strengthen a regional culture of computing, algorithms, and academic competitions. LPPC starts from that possibility with a simple proposal: to create a public, serious, and accessible practice routine.

The long-term view is to form a community capable of supporting teams, beginning students, and more experienced participants. This growth should happen gradually, without exaggerated promises and without dependence on a single institution.
